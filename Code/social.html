Indian Constitution


The Indian Constitution is the country's supreme law. The constitution establishes a framework that defines the fundamental political code, structure, procedures, powers, and responsibilities of government institutions, as well as people' fundamental rights, directive principles, and responsibilities. It also is the longest constitution in the world containing 449 articles in 25 parts, 12 schedules, 5 appendices and 104 Amendments. It decides what is right and what is wrong. Without the constitution there wouldn't be fair judgement. A democratic country needs a constitution because It provides useful information about the government's structure, organs, tasks, and responsibilities.



    Fundamental Rights and Duties


    There are 6 fundamental rights to every citizen of India:

  • Right to Equality

    • Every person is treated equally before law and is guaranteed a fair trail in the court. No one can be prejudiced against because of their caste, gender, colour, or religion.

  • Right to Freedom

    • The right to freedom allows citizens basic freedoms including such freedom of speech and expression, the ability to establish organizations, individual freedoms, as well as the right to sustain a dignified life.

  • Right against Exploitation

    • People are protected from human trafficking and compulsory labour under this Fundamental Right. It also forbids children under the age of 14 from engaging in dangerous jobs or facilities.

  • Right to Freedom of Religion

    • The religious liberty, religion, and worship are all included in the giving of this right. Any religion can be followed by anyone. It ensures that all religions have the freedom to form and sustain religious institutions. No one can be obliged to pay a tax in spreading a religion. The state cannot impose a tax on any religion, and religious education in schools and institutions is banned by the constitution.

  • Cultural and Educational Rights

    • The Constitution ensures minorities' rights to preserve and promote their languages and cultures in this category. It also gives them the power to establish, administer, and control own educational institutions.

  • Right to Constitutional Remedies

    • The Bill of Rights is based around this basic aspect.If a citizen feels his or her rights have been violated, he or she may pursue justice in a courtroom. It provides judicial enforcement and protection of fundamental rights. It provides the Supreme Court and the High Courts the authority to issue writs to enforce these rights.


The 11 fundamental duties of every citizen of India


1) Honor for the National Anthem and Flag in line with the Indian Constitution

2) To cherish and preserve the noble ideals that inspired the nation's fight for independence.

3) To protect India's integrity, sovereignty, and unity

4) When and if the nation needs it, to defend the nation and perform national services.

5) To promote a spirit of fraternity and harmony among all Indians, and to oppose any practises that are demeaning to women.

6) To cherish and safeguard our composite culture's unique national heritage

7) To safeguard and enhance the natural environment, which contains lakes, wildlife, rivers, and forests, among many other things.

8) To develop a scientific mindset, humanism, and curiosity

9) To maintain all public property safe

10) To strive for perfection in all kinds of personal and collective activity.

11) To provide educational opportunities for all children from 6 to 14, and that it is the responsibility of the parents to ensure that such opportunities are supplied to their kids.



Parliament of India


The President and two Houses, known as the Council of States (Rajya Sabha) and House of the People, make up the Union's legislature, which is known as Parliament (Lok Sabha). Each House is expected to assemble within 6 months of the previous session. In some situations, a simultaneous sitting of both Houses is possible.

Judiciary of India


The judiciary's primary responsibility is to uphold the rule of law and ensure its supremacy. Personal rights are respected, disputes are handled according to the law, and democracy does not give way to person or group rule.

Executive of India


The President, his or her advisors, and various organizational units make up the executive branch. The implementation of the laws of this country is the duty of this branch. Cabinet of the President (Federal Agencies) Independent Federal Commissions and Agencies.

The Parliament has three core functions to implement which is to act on behalf the interests of people, pass laws and to monitor the actions of the government. Now since the elected people are placed in house lok sabha and rajya sabha they discuss and pass new laws based on the people's interest and they also monitor how the law is being implemented.





Bills

A Bill is a proposed statute that becomes law after both Houses of Parliament pass it and the President signs it. Bills are the vehicles through which all legislative proposals are submitted to Parliament. The total bills passed by both the house in all time is nearly 2525.

Agriculture and Civilizations



When people first started pursuing agriculture about ten thousand years ago, not only did their lives change, but the Earth's landscape did as well. Humans were no longer compelled to walk around in quest of meat. They could settle down in one location and produce more food than they need, leading in an increase in human population. Humans also made more deliberate changes to the Earth's surface in order to increase the amount of land available for cultivation.Humans made the land more suitable for growing crops by cutting down trees and digging irrigation canals, to give some examples. Agriculture is the seventh major threshold of increasing complexity in this course because it resulted in such dramatic changes in the Earth and the way humans lived. Scientists and historians, as well as the rest of us, are still discovering more about the origins of agriculture and the implications of this evolution for our species. This now is proving that farming is much better than foraging both for humans as well as the land and animals
